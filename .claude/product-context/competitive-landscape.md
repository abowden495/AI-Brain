# Competitive Landscape: Liminal Networking App

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-14
**Product:** Liminal Networking App (curated buyer community for identity/fraud/cybersecurity)
**Status:** PRD v0.4 under review, pre-Tollgate 1

---

## Direct Competitors (Buyer-Side CISO/Cybersecurity Communities)

### The CISO Society
- **Type:** Invite-only, vendor-neutral peer community for CISOs
- **Size:** Curated (not publicly disclosed)
- **Model:** Peer-driven, private Slack under Chatham House rules
- **Buyer Focus:** Yes -- explicitly helps members navigate vendor purchasing
- **Identity/Fraud Specific:** No (broad cybersecurity)
- **Threat Level:** High -- closest to Liminal's proposed model
- **Source:** https://www.thecisosociety.com/

### CyberRisk Collaborative (CRC)
- **Type:** Subscription-based, vendor-free CISO community
- **Size:** 3,000+ members
- **Model:** Premium membership with vendor-free decision support, working groups
- **Buyer Focus:** Yes -- vendor-free strategy validation
- **Identity/Fraud Specific:** No (broad cybersecurity)
- **Threat Level:** High -- scaled buyer community already operating
- **Source:** https://www.cyberriskcollaborative.com/

### Evanta/Gartner C-Level CISO Communities
- **Type:** Vendor-sponsored executive summits + year-round peer communities
- **Size:** 3,600+ CISOs in 34 communities globally
- **Model:** Vendor sponsorship with governing body oversight; free for CISOs
- **Buyer Focus:** Yes -- matched by purchasing priorities
- **Identity/Fraud Specific:** No (broad C-level)
- **Threat Level:** Medium-High -- well-funded, Gartner-backed
- **Acquisition:** Evanta acquired by CEB/Gartner for $275M in 2016
- **Source:** https://www.evanta.com/ciso

### RSAC Year-Round Community
- **Type:** Year-round membership platform (launched 2025)
- **Size:** Large (RSA Conference attendee base)
- **Model:** Community membership with secure messaging, groups, AI co-pilot
- **Buyer Focus:** Partially
- **Identity/Fraud Specific:** No (broad cybersecurity)
- **Threat Level:** High -- well-resourced, largest cybersecurity brand, expanding year-round
- **Source:** https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/rsac-expands-beyond-its-flagship-cybersecurity-conference-unveils-new-community-centric-cybersecurity-membership-platform-302417646.html

## Comparable Models (Non-Competing)

### Hampton
- **ARR:** ~$8M from ~1,000 members at $8,500/year
- **Acceptance Rate:** ~8%
- **Retention Driver:** In-person 8-person Core Groups meeting 10x/year with paid moderators
- **Key Lesson:** Value comes from small group facilitation, not digital platform

### Pavilion
- **ARR:** ~$11M from 10,000+ members
- **Pricing:** $1,375-$5,000+/year tiered
- **Growth:** Word-of-mouth driven
- **Key Lesson:** Works because GTM leader universe is huge; identity/fraud universe is orders of magnitude smaller

### YPO
- **Size:** Global, multi-decade
- **Retention:** 85% target
- **Key Driver:** 8-person forums meeting monthly + lifetime membership path
- **Key Lesson:** Small group trust + lifelong design = decades of retention

### Vistage
- **Size:** 45,000+ members, 40 countries
- **Key Driver:** 12-16 person groups with professional Chair facilitation
- **Key Lesson:** Structured accountability and professional facilitation drive sustained engagement

## Substitute Behaviors (What Buyers Do Today)

1. **Private Signal/WhatsApp/Slack groups** -- Majority of CISOs participate; free, habitual, invisible
2. **Conference conversations** -- RSA, Identiverse, Money20/20, LCS: 2-3 concentrated bursts/year
3. **LinkedIn DMs** -- Noisy but habitual for many buyers
4. **Analyst calls** -- Gartner inquiries, Liminal research calls
5. **Doing nothing** -- Many buyers comfortable with limited peer input

---

## White Space (Liminal's Actual Differentiation)

**What is NOT true:** "No buyer-side community exists in identity/fraud/cybersecurity"
**What IS true:** No community exists for the specific identity/fraud/cyber buyer persona (CPO/CSO/CEO) with Liminal's combination of:
1. Vertical depth in identity verification + fraud prevention
2. Existing trust density from LCS and years of relationships
3. Market intelligence context from Command platform
4. Curated buyer-only environment at decision-maker level

## Failure Pattern Analysis

### Clubhouse
- Pandemic-dependent, lost exclusivity, no verification, no monetization, incumbent competition

### Lunchclub ($28-56M raised)
- Match quality degraded at scale, no user control, support failures, novelty decay

### Chief
- Scaled to 20K, faced churn concerns, employer budget cuts impacted renewals, restructured 2023

### Soho House
- Scaled to 45 locations/185K members, exclusivity diluted, never profitable in 30 years, going private

---

## Key Competitive Risks

1. RSAC year-round community platform expanding into vertical tracks
2. CISO Society and CRC already serving vendor-neutral buyer peer needs
3. Existing private Signal/WhatsApp groups are free and habitual
4. "Community fatigue" -- CISOs already in multiple communities
5. Gartner/Evanta has $275M+ invested in this model with 3,600 CISOs
