# Strategic Goals

**Period:** Q1 2026
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-12

## Product Vision

By 2029, Liminal is the intelligence operating system for the global Identity, Fraud, and Cybersecurity market -- the verified source of truth where every strategic decision and revenue action in the industry begins.

## Strategic Priorities

1. **Deepen enterprise account value and expansion.** Move from single-team adoption to multi-team intelligence workflows within existing accounts (Visa, Mastercard, Google, JPMC). Success means strategy leaders AND revenue leaders at the same company are running on Liminal daily, creating organizational lock-in through shared intelligence -- not just individual tool usage.

2. **Prove agentic capabilities deliver measurable customer outcomes.** Coach AI, Insights Agent, Target Account Lists, and Team Spaces must demonstrate concrete ROI -- faster deal cycles, earlier pattern detection, higher conversion on targeted accounts. The bar is not "users like it" but "users can quantify the value." This validates the next phase of product investment and pricing expansion.

3. **Launch the Buyer Application to compound the Living Graph moat and unlock a two-sided intelligence network.** Extend Liminal from a single-sided vendor intelligence platform to a two-sided market by shipping the Buyer Application — an embedded observation widget that gives enterprise buyers (Stack Owners at JPMC, Candescent, and similar) a unified health score across their risk stack, with a bridge into Command for deeper verified context. This creates a proprietary data flywheel: anonymized buyer performance signals flow into the Living Graph, enriching vendor-side intelligence in Command and GTM, while vendor-side market structure flows back to buyers as benchmarks. The buyer app is the architectural foundation for the next phase of growth, expanding SAM from $2–3B to $9.3–14B using data already in the graph. MVP (Layer 1: Health Check) targets Q2 co-development with JPMC, first revenue $250K ARR in Q4 2026.

## Strategic Rationale

### Why these priorities (and not others)

**What we are NOT doing this quarter:**
- Not pursuing horizontal market expansion (e.g., adjacent verticals beyond Identity/Fraud/Cyber). The market is large enough and PMF is specific to this domain. Expanding too early dilutes positioning.
- Not investing in self-serve or PLG motion. Enterprise accounts are the growth engine. The Analyst Desk and high-touch model is a feature, not a bug -- it is what makes intelligence "verified."
- Not building new agentic capabilities beyond the buyer app. The focus is proving existing agentic features deliver outcomes while concentrating new product investment on the buyer-side market expansion.

**Key trade-off:** Depth over breadth. We are choosing to go deeper with fewer, larger accounts rather than scaling the number of accounts. This builds the expansion playbook and case studies needed to scale efficiently later.

### Positioning Summary

- **For:** Strategy leaders and revenue leaders at companies operating in Identity, Fraud, and Cybersecurity
- **Who:** Cannot afford decisions based on unverified AI outputs, stale analyst reports, or disconnected point solutions
- **Liminal is:** A verified intelligence operating system
- **That:** Unifies strategic decision-making and revenue execution on a single source of truth, with agentic intelligence that surfaces patterns before they become obvious
- **Unlike:** Generic AI tools (unverified, no domain depth), traditional analyst firms (stale, slow, not actionable), point solutions (disconnected, no graph)
- **We:** Deliver intelligence grounded in a proprietary Living Graph of 2.5M+ verified entities, validated by human analysts, and activated through agentic workflows that compress months of work into hours

### North Star Metric

**Time from market signal to customer action** -- How quickly can a Liminal user go from "something changed in the market" to "we made a decision or took a revenue action based on verified intelligence." This captures the full value chain: graph freshness, intelligence quality, agentic delivery, and user activation.
