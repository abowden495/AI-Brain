Completed critical research methodology review of Liminal Networking App PRD v0.4 on 2026-03-14.

**Key findings:**
- Tollgate 2 (10-15 interviews, 80% commit gate) is vulnerable to social desirability bias since sample is warm LCS contacts with existing Liminal relationships
- Tollgate 3 (concierge with 30-50 hand-picked members) risks survivorship bias and conflating concierge service value with product value
- NPS 45+ from white-glove group is not informative; the bar of 5+ useful introductions from 30-50 people is too low
- Major research gaps: willingness to pay, trust/data sensitivity validation, competitive alternatives (what buyers do today), and engagement pattern realism for senior executives

**Recommendations provided:**
- Three-tier sample design: warm LCS core, peripheral contacts, and buyers with NO Liminal relationship
- Behavioral commitment scoring (calendar slots, named contacts, specific benchmarking questions) instead of verbal commitment
- Pre-registered success criteria before interviews
- Concierge cost-per-engagement tracking
- Denominator transparency (% of original cohort, not % of active members)

**Why:** The biggest risk is the team validates what they want to hear via relational goodwill rather than genuine product demand.

**How to apply:** Reference this when any follow-up research planning, interview guide creation, or tollgate review is requested for the networking app.
