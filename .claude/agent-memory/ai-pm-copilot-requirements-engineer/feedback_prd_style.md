Andrew explicitly asked for PRDs to be "honest" — clearly flagging what is validated vs. assumed, and not papering over failed past attempts or unresolved questions.

**Why:** The networking app concept closely parallels LAN (a prior failed initiative). The request was to call that out directly rather than ignore it.

**How to apply:**
- Always include a "Validated vs. Assumed" section for early-stage or speculative features
- Explicitly call out past failures or analogues when they exist in the meeting notes or context
- Use a visible honesty/status note at the top of draft PRDs to signal they are brainstorm-derived, not committed plans
- Open questions section should be a decision-blocking list with owners and status, not just a footnote
- Evidence in PRDs should be attributed to source (meeting session, context file, user-provided data) — never fabricated
